# Research Summary: Zagreb/Croatia Market for Small Computer Repair and IT Help Services

## Context and positioning

This research is intended for a Croatian landing page for a **small local IT help service in Zagreb**. The strongest market opportunity is not to look like a large technical repair company, but to sound like a calm, competent local person who explains things plainly, gives realistic prices, and admits when a job is outside scope.

Your proposed positioning is well aligned with a gap in the market: **honest, direct, affordable but not suspiciously cheap, beginner-friendly, transparent, personal, and willing to say “I cannot fix this” when appropriate**. Many competitors emphasize speed, years of experience, technical depth, or large service scope. Fewer communicate in a simple, human way for ordinary home users, older users, freelancers, students, and small offices who just want help without feeling stupid.

## 1. Common services offered by Zagreb computer/laptop repair providers

Across Zagreb and Croatian IT repair pages, the most common services fall into a few predictable groups. A small local landing page does not need to offer all of them. It should focus on the services that match your actual skill, tools, availability, and risk tolerance.

| Service group | Common services seen in the market | Fit for a small beginner-friendly IT help service |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic computer help | Slow computer, setup help, software problems, program installation, printer/scanner setup, email/account setup, advice | Very good fit, especially if described in plain language. |
| Operating system work | Windows installation/reinstallation, Windows 10/11 upgrade, Linux/macOS installation in some shops, driver setup | Good fit if you handle licensing and backups carefully. |
| Data and backup | Backup before reinstall, data transfer, disk cloning, simple file recovery, backup setup | Good fit for basic transfer/backup; risky for advanced recovery. |
| Hardware upgrades | SSD installation, RAM upgrade, desktop component installation, PC assembly | Good fit if parts and compatibility are clearly handled. |
| Cleaning and overheating | Dust cleaning, fan cleaning, thermal paste replacement, gaming laptop cleaning | Good fit if you have tools and explain limits; laptop disassembly can carry risk. |
| Virus and security | Virus/malware removal, antivirus setup, basic security check, browser cleanup | Good fit if you avoid promising complete security. |
| Network and home devices | Wi‑Fi/router setup, printer connection, smart TV or device setup, home-office setup | Strong fit for beginner-friendly positioning. |
| Remote support | Udaljena podrška, remote troubleshooting, short advice sessions | Strong fit, especially for small problems and transparent minimum pricing. |
| Field/home service | Dolazak na adresu, terenski rad, call-out fee, “kod kuće” help | Good differentiator if available in Zagreb neighborhoods. |
| Advanced repairs | Screen replacement, keyboard replacement, hinge repair, power connector repair, motherboard repair, BGA/SMD soldering, liquid damage | Offer only if truly capable; otherwise refer out honestly. |
| Business IT | Maintenance contracts, system administration, small-office support, backup solutions | Possible later, but do not overemphasize if landing page targets individuals. |

The most natural scope for your positioning is **everyday IT help**, not advanced electronics repair. Your strongest services are likely to be: Windows cleanup/reinstall, SSD/RAM upgrade coordination, data transfer, printer/Wi‑Fi setup, remote help, beginner lessons, slow-computer diagnosis, malware cleanup, backup setup, and honest referral for complex hardware or board-level repairs.

## 2. Common pricing patterns

Zagreb providers commonly use a mix of **fixed prices**, **hourly rates**, **diagnostic fees**, **call-out fees**, and **“po ponudi / na upit”** for complex jobs. The market is not uniform, but several patterns are clear.

| Pricing item | Observed market range | Notes for your landing page |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Basic hourly service | 25–40 EUR/hour | 25 EUR feels affordable; 30–35 EUR feels normal and credible; 40 EUR is common for field or more specialized work. |
| Remote support | 25 EUR/hour or small short-session fee | A 15–20 minute “quick help” option can feel approachable. |
| Field/home visit | 10–20 EUR call-out plus hourly work, or 30–40 EUR/hour field rate | Be very clear whether travel is included, separate, or neighborhood-dependent. |
| Diagnostic | Free if repair is performed; otherwise 20–40 EUR | A transparent diagnostic policy builds trust. |
| Windows reinstall | 25–55 EUR | Higher price usually includes backup or more setup. Separate “without backup” and “with backup” if needed. |
| Windows reinstall with backup | Around 45–60 EUR | Do not include unlimited backup in a fixed price unless capped. |
| SSD installation / disk cloning | 30 EUR common for labor; parts separate | Explain that SSD cost is not included. |
| Laptop/PC cleaning | Desktop roughly 15–35 EUR; laptop roughly 25–50 EUR; gaming laptop 40–60 EUR | Good fixed-price service, but note that complicated models can cost more. |
| Virus removal / optimization | 20–50 EUR | Avoid promising that every infection can be fully removed without reinstall. |
| Printer/router/peripheral setup | Often hourly or 25–60 EUR depending on complexity | Good candidate for a simple local package. |
| Data backup/transfer | 10 EUR for small capped backup to 30+ EUR for disk cloning; recovery “na upit” | Separate normal transfer from real data recovery. |
| Advanced hardware repair | Often “po ponudi” or 30–65 EUR/hour; board-level can be much higher | Do not compete here unless equipped. |

The best price positioning for your brand is **not the cheapest**. Very low prices can look outdated or suspicious. A credible simple structure would be: a small diagnostic/consultation fee, a normal hourly rate, fixed starter packages, and clear statements that parts are charged separately.

## 4. What competitor websites often do badly

The most common competitor weakness is not lack of technical skill; it is weak communication. Many sites are written for search engines or technically confident users rather than worried ordinary people.

| Weakness | Why it matters | Opportunity for your service |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Too much technical language | Beginners may not know whether their problem is “driver”, “BIOS”, “BGA”, “Level 2”, or “board-level”. | Explain problems in everyday Croatian first, then mention technical details only when useful. |
| Long, repetitive copy | Some pages repeat urgency and problem statements many times, which can feel old-fashioned or manipulative. | Keep sections short, calm, and direct. |
| Dated design and broken diacritics | Old layouts, encoding errors, typos, and inconsistent Croatian reduce trust. | Use clean typography, correct Croatian diacritics, and simple structure. |
| Unclear pricing logic | Some pages list many prices but do not explain what is included or excluded. | Publish a small set of understandable packages and a clear “parts not included” note. |
| “Na upit” everywhere | Users cannot estimate whether they can afford help. | Use realistic ranges where exact pricing is impossible. |
| Overpromising speed | Claims like immediate service, always urgent, or same-day repair can create disappointment. | Say what is realistic: “Ako sam slobodan, mogu pomoći isti ili sljedeći dan.” |
| Too corporate or impersonal | Large retailers and advanced repair shops can feel intimidating. | Show that this is a small local service with a real person behind it. |
| Legal disclaimers only at the end | Important limits about data, warranty, parts, and software are often hidden or written harshly. | Present limits early and politely: “Prije rada dogovorimo što se može, što se ne može i koliko bi moglo koštati.” |
| Broad service lists | Offering everything from laptop repair to web design, CCTV, servers, and electronics can look unfocused. | Narrow the landing page to the services your ideal customer actually needs. |

## 5. How your service can feel more human and trustworthy

Your service can stand out by sounding less like a repair factory and more like a reliable local helper. The key is to combine warmth with boundaries.

| Trust element | Recommended approach |
| --- | --- |
| Personal introduction | Use a short “Tko sam ja” section with your first name, area of Zagreb served, and the type of people you help. |
| Plain-language promise | Say that you explain the problem in normal language before doing paid work. |
| No-pressure pricing | State that the customer gets an estimate before work continues. |
| Honest limits | Say directly that you do not pretend to fix everything, and that you will recommend another option if repair is not worthwhile. |
| Beginner-friendly tone | Include wording like “Možete pitati i osnovna pitanja” and “Nema potrebe da znate tehničke izraze.” |
| Transparent exclusions | Make clear that parts, licenses, advanced data recovery, and specialist electronics repair are not included in basic prices. |
| Respect for data | Explain that customers should back up important data when possible, and that you handle files carefully but cannot guarantee data recovery. |
| Local proof | Mention neighborhoods served, examples of common problems, simple reviews/testimonials once available, and real contact details. |
| Human contact | Offer phone, WhatsApp, and a short contact form. Make it easy to describe the problem in one sentence. |
| Calm urgency | Offer “hitna pomoć ako sam dostupan” rather than claiming instant availability. |

A strong message for your brand would be: **“Ako mogu riješiti problem, reći ću vam kako i koliko će otprilike koštati. Ako ne mogu, reći ću vam to odmah — bez naplaćivanja nepotrebnog rada.”** This sentence is an original positioning suggestion and should be adapted to your actual policy.

## 6. Suggested landing page sections

The landing page should be shorter and more reassuring than most competitor pages. It should help a non-technical visitor decide within 30 seconds whether to contact you.

| Section | Purpose | Suggested Croatian heading |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hero | Explain who you help, where, and why you are different. | **IT pomoć u Zagrebu — jasno, pošteno i bez kompliciranja** |
| Problem examples | Let users recognize their issue without technical vocabulary. | **S čime vam mogu pomoći?** |
| Services | List only core services, grouped simply. | **Najčešće usluge** |
| How it works | Reduce uncertainty and fear of hidden costs. | **Kako izgleda pomoć?** |
| Prices/packages | Show transparent starting prices and ranges. | **Okvirne cijene** |
| Honest limits | Build trust by saying what you do not promise. | **Što mogu, a što ne obećavam** |
| About | Make the service personal and small. | **Tko vam pomaže** |
| Beginner reassurance | Address shame/fear directly. | **Nema glupih pitanja** |
| Service area | Capture local SEO and expectations. | **Zagreb i okolica / dolazak na adresu** |
| FAQ | Handle price, data, parts, timing, remote support, payment. | **Česta pitanja** |

The page should not start with a long technical list. It should start with the user’s emotional situation: the computer is slow, Windows is confusing, printer does not work, Wi‑Fi is unstable, files need transferring, or they simply need somebody patient.

## 8. Risks: what you should not promise

Your honest positioning will be credible only if the landing page avoids risky promises. The safest rule is to promise your **process**, not the final outcome.

| Do not promise | Safer alternative |
| --- | --- |
| “Popravit ću svaki kvar.” | “Pregledat ću problem i reći vam što je realno moguće.” |
| “Spašavam sve podatke.” | “Mogu pomoći s osnovnim backupom i prijenosom podataka; za ozbiljan kvar diska preporučit ću specijalizirani servis.” |
| “Dolazim odmah.” | “Ako sam dostupan, mogu pomoći isti ili sljedeći dan.” |
| “Najjeftiniji servis u Zagrebu.” | “Poštene i jasne cijene bez nepotrebnog rada.” |
| “Računalo će biti kao novo.” | “Uklonit ću nepotrebne probleme i predložiti nadogradnju ako ima smisla.” |
| “Uklanjam sve viruse zauvijek.” | “Očistit ću sustav koliko je moguće i objasniti kako smanjiti rizik ubuduće.” |
| “Bez rizika za podatke.” | “S podacima postupam pažljivo, ali preporučujem backup prije svakog većeg zahvata.” |
| “Jamstvo na cijelo računalo.” | “Jamstvo se može odnositi samo na konkretan obavljeni rad ili ugrađeni dio.” |
| “Besplatna dijagnostika” if not always true | “Kratka početna procjena je besplatna; detaljna dijagnostika se naplaćuje ako ne nastavljamo s radom.” |
| “Fiksna cijena za sve slučajeve.” | “Za česte usluge imam okvirne cijene, a za složenije slučajeve šaljem procjenu.” |

You should also avoid implying that you can legally install paid software without a license. Use wording such as **“instalacija programa uz vašu licencu ili besplatnu legalnu alternativu”**.

## Recommended final positioning

The most defensible landing-page angle is: **small, local, patient IT help for normal people in Zagreb**. Do not compete with large shops on advanced electronics repair, original parts, corporate service contracts, or “instant” emergency claims. Compete on clarity, kindness, and honest boundaries.

A concise Croatian positioning statement could be:

> **IT pomoć u Zagrebu za ljude koji žele jasno objašnjenje, poštenu cijenu i rješenje bez nepotrebnog kompliciranja. Ako problem mogu riješiti, reći ću vam kako. Ako ne mogu ili se popravak ne isplati, reći ću vam to odmah.**

## Practical landing-page copy direction

Use a calm and direct tone. Write in the first person singular if the service is truly personal. Avoid sounding like a large anonymous company if that is not what you are.

| Page element | Recommended direction |
| --- | --- |
| Voice | First person singular or small-service plural, but be consistent. For your positioning, first person singular may feel more trustworthy. |
| Tone | Simple, patient, transparent, not overly technical. |
| Croatian style | Use correct diacritics and standard phrasing: “s računalom”, “prijenosno računalo/laptop”, “sigurnosna kopija”, “podešavanje”, “dijagnostika”. |
| CTA | “Pošaljite mi kratko što se događa” or “Opišite problem u jednoj poruci”. |
| Pricing | Show 5–7 understandable packages, not a giant technical table. |

| Limits | Include a friendly “Ne radim sve” section. This is a strength, not a weakness. |

## Suggested minimum landing-page structure

If you want a concise page, use this order:

1. **Hero:** IT pomoć u Zagrebu — jasno, pošteno i bez kompliciranja.
2. **Common problems:** sporo računalo, Windows problemi, printer/Wi‑Fi, backup, prijenos podataka, postavljanje uređaja.
3. **How it works:** pošaljete poruku, dobijete procjenu, radim tek nakon dogovora, kažem ako nema smisla popravljati.
4. **Packages/prices:** 5–7 simple packages with ranges.
5. **Honest limits:** no fake promises, no illegal software, no guaranteed data recovery, no advanced electronics unless actually offered.
6. **About:** small local service, real person, beginner-friendly.
7. **FAQ:** data, parts, licenses, home visit, remote help, payment.
8. **Contact:** phone/WhatsApp/email form with “opišite problem u jednoj rečenici”.

## Bottom line

The Zagreb market already has many providers for **servis računala**, **servis laptopa**, and advanced repair. Your best niche is not to appear bigger than you are. Your best niche is to be **more understandable, more honest, and less intimidating** than the average repair site.

The landing page should make visitors feel: “This person will not make me feel stupid, will not hide the price, and will tell me honestly whether the repair makes sense.”
